August 2002 - May 2012
For those unfamiliar with the Montessori Method, it is a student-oriented approach to early education developed by Italian scientist, Maria Montessori, in the early 1900s. The program promotes the organic adoption of values such as independence, curiosity, and enthusiasm through a self-paced and individualized curriculum. Many age ranges will share the same space to encourage community and accountability. The Montessori method is not suitable for everyone since there is very little structure, but I was able to flourish in the program. I attribute my excitement for learning and pursuance of STEM subjects to the freedom I had in the classroom through 9th grade. The community focus of Montessori also helped me adopt diversity as a core value moving into High School.
