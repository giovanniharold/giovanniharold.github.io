August 2002 - May 2012
For those unfamiliar with the Montessori Method, it is a student-oriented approach to early education developed by Italian scientist Maria Montessori in the early 1900s. The program promotes the organic adoption of values such as independence, curiosity, and enthusiasm through a self-paced and individualized curriculum. Many age ranges will share the same space to encourage community and accountability. The Montessori method is not suitable for everyone, there is very little structure, but I was able to flourish in the program. I attribute my excitement for learning and pursuance of STEM subjects to the freedom I had in the classroom through 9th grade. The community focus of Montessori also helped me adopt diversity as a core value moving into High School.

Through LFA, I strengthened my understanding of the importance of diversity by developing an appreciation for seeing the differences in individual's backgrounds. Summer 2016
Banner Day Camp is a summer camp in Lake Forest, Illinois, that highlights the importance of diversity, inclusion, and self-awareness. Every team member, all the way to counselors, gets high-quality training to ensure every camper has a rewarding and developmental experience. While working at Banner, I had a group of 16 first-grade boys for four-week intervals or one eight-week duration. Having campers assigned to one counselor helps ensure that the camper's individual needs, such as allergy accommodation or medical care, do not slip through the cracks. I also volunteered to be on the extended-day crew, five counselors, who stayed at camp each day and continued activities to accommodate parents who wouldn't be home for the bus service. August 2017 - March 2019
Morey is a contract electronics manufacturing facility that prioritizes quality and customer service. While attending Purdue University, I spent rotating semesters at Morey as a team member in the High Volume department. The experience was rewarding since it complimented my coursework as I pursued an Industrial Engineering degree. My responsibilities included defect monitoring, identifying process improvements, and participating in/leading Kaizen events. Concepts that I learned at Purdue allowed me to excel in those areas, but the Co-op also prepared me for working in a professional environment.
